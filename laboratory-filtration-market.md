<p><p>Laboratory filtration refers to the process of separating solids from liquids in a laboratory setting. There are several types of filtration methods used, including ultrafiltration, microfiltration, nanofiltration, and reverse osmosis. Ultrafiltration is used to separate particles based on size, while microfiltration is used to remove bacteria and large particles. Nanofiltration removes smaller particles and organic substances, and reverse osmosis is a more advanced technique that removes almost all impurities from a liquid by applying pressure to force it through a membrane.</p></p>
