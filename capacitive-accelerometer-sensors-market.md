<p><p>Capacitive accelerometers are sensors that measure acceleration by detecting changes in capacitance within the sensor. These sensors are classified based on the number of axes they can measure: 1-axis, 2-axis, and 3-axis. 1-axis sensors measure acceleration along a single plane, 2-axis sensors measure acceleration along two perpendicular planes, and 3-axis sensors can measure acceleration along all three axes. Each type of sensor is used in various applications where precise measurement of acceleration is required in different directions.</p></p>
