<p><p>Railway transit cables are divided into two main types: Communication Cables and Power Cables. Communication cables are used for transmitting signals and data along the railway network, enabling communication between control centers, stations, and trains. Power cables, on the other hand, are responsible for supplying electricity to various components of the railway system, including signaling systems, lighting, and traction motors. Both types of cables play a critical role in ensuring the safe and efficient operation of railway transit systems.</p></p>
