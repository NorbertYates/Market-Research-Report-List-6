<p><p>Smart power sockets are classified into three main types based on connectivity: Wi-Fi, Bluetooth, and other technologies. Wi-Fi sockets connect directly to home networks, allowing remote access and control via mobile apps or voice assistants. Bluetooth sockets offer short-range connectivity, enabling users to manage power settings locally without internet dependency. Other technologies may include Zigbee or Z-Wave, which facilitate smart home integration while maintaining low energy consumption. Each type caters to different user needs regarding range, convenience, and network requirements.</p></p>
