<p><p>Cryopreservation systems are used to store biological samples at low temperatures to maintain their viability. Blood refrigerators are designed to store blood products at a temperature of 4℃ to preserve their quality. Medical refrigerators are used to store vaccines and other medical supplies at a temperature range of 2℃-8℃. Biomedical freezers are used for long-term storage of biological samples at temperatures ranging from -10℃ to -50℃. Ultra-low freezers can reach temperatures as low as -150℃ and are commonly used for storing cells and tissues. Liquid nitrogen tanks are used for storing samples at extremely low temperatures, ranging from -150℃ to -196℃. Other types of cryopreservation systems include cryogenic storage systems and controlled-rate freezers, which are used for specific applications in research and medical laboratories.</p></p>
