<p><p>The GP vaccination market consists of different types of vaccines, including DNA vaccines, non-replicating viral vector vaccines, protein subunit vaccines, RNA vaccines, and others. DNA vaccines use genetically engineered DNA to stimulate an immune response. Non-replicating viral vector vaccines use a virus as a vector to deliver genetic material and trigger an immune response. Protein subunit vaccines contain purified proteins from the virus. RNA vaccines use RNA to produce viral proteins and induce an immune response. Other types of vaccines in this market may include inactivated or live-attenuated vaccines.</p></p>
