<p><p>Surfactants are classified into four main types based on their chemical composition and charge: anionic surfactants, amphoteric surfactants, cationic surfactants, and nonionic surfactants. Anionic surfactants are negatively charged and commonly used in laundry detergents. Amphoteric surfactants have both positive and negative charges, making them versatile in various applications. Cationic surfactants are positively charged and are often used in fabric softeners. Nonionic surfactants are uncharged and commonly used in personal care products. Each type of surfactant has unique properties and applications in the market.</p></p>
