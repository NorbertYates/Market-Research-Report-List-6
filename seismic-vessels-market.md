<p><p>Seismic vessels are categorized into two main types based on the water depth in which they operate: Shallow Water and Deep Water. Shallow water seismic vessels are designed to operate in water depths of up to 200 meters, making them suitable for conducting seismic surveys closer to the shore. In contrast, Deep Water seismic vessels are capable of operating in water depths exceeding 200 meters, allowing them to conduct surveys in deeper offshore areas. Both types of vessels play a crucial role in exploring and mapping subsurface geological structures for oil and gas exploration.</p></p>
