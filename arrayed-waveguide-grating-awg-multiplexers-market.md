<p><p>Arrayed Waveguide Grating (AWG) multiplexers are devices used in optical communication systems to combine or separate multiple signals with different wavelengths. There are two main types of AWG multiplexers: Thermal AWG and Athermal AWG. </p><p>Thermal AWGs require temperature stabilization to maintain precise wavelength alignment, while Athermal AWGs utilize advanced materials to automatically compensate for temperature fluctuations. Thermal AWGs are commonly used in applications where temperature control is feasible, while Athermal AWGs are preferred for environments with varying temperatures.</p></p>
