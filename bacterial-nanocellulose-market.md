<p><strong>The Bacterial Nanocellulose Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Nanofibrillated Cellulose</li><li>Nanocrystalline Cellulose</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The bacterial nanocellulose market is primarily divided into two types: nanofibrillated cellulose (NFC) and nanocrystalline cellulose (NCC). NFC is characterized by its long, flexible fibers derived from bacterial sources, offering high viscosity and excellent mechanical properties, making it suitable for applications in food, cosmetics, and biomedical products. NCC, on the other hand, consists of short, crystalline nanoparticles that provide reinforcement and enhance thermal stability, commonly used in packaging, coatings, and composites. Both types leverage their unique properties for diverse industrial applications.</p></p>
