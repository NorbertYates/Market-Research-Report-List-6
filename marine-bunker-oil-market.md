<p><p>Marine bunker oil refers to the fuel used by ships for propulsion. There are two main types of marine bunker oil: residual fuel and distillate fuel. Residual fuel is a heavier, more viscous oil that is left over after the refining process, while distillate fuel is a lighter, cleaner oil that is produced through distillation. Residual fuel is typically used in large, ocean-going vessels, while distillate fuel is used in smaller ships and boats. Both types of fuel are essential for the functioning of the marine industry.</p></p>
