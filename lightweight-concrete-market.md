<p><p>Lightweight concrete is a type of concrete that utilizes lightweight aggregates to reduce its density, making it lighter than traditional concrete. Three common types of lightweight concrete are Lightweight Aggregate Concrete, which uses lightweight aggregates like expanded clay or shale; Aerated Concrete, which contains air bubbles to reduce its weight; and No Fines Concrete, which has a lower proportion of fine aggregates to achieve a lighter composition. These types of lightweight concrete offer various benefits, such as improved thermal insulation, reduced structural load, and ease of handling during construction.</p></p>
