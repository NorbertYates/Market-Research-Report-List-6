<p><p>Industrial abrasives can be divided into two main types: metallic abrasives and nonmetallic abrasives. Metallic abrasives are typically made of steel grit or shot and are commonly used for cutting and shaping hard materials such as metal, concrete, and stone. Nonmetallic abrasives, on the other hand, are made from materials like silicon carbide, aluminum oxide, or garnet and are used for polishing, grinding, and sanding applications on softer materials such as wood, plastic, and ceramics. Both types play a crucial role in various industries for material removal and finishing purposes.</p></p>
