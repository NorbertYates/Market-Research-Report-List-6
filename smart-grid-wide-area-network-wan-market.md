<p><p>Smart Grid Wide Area Network (WAN) market includes two main types: Wired WAN and Wireless WAN. Wired WAN refers to networks that use physical cables, such as fiber optic or Ethernet, to connect devices over long distances. On the other hand, Wireless WAN utilizes radio waves or satellite links for communication, offering more flexibility and mobility. Both markets cater to the need for reliable and secure communication infrastructures in smart grid systems, but they differ in terms of cost, speed, and installation complexity.</p></p>
