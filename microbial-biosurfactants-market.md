<p><p>The microbial biosurfactants market comprises various types, including rhamnolipids and sophorolipids. Rhamnolipids, produced by Pseudomonas spp., exhibit excellent surface-active properties, making them effective in applications like bioremediation and enhanced oil recovery. Sophorolipids, derived from yeast, are known for their biodegradability and eco-friendliness, primarily used in cosmetics and cleaning products. Other biosurfactants include mannosylerythritol lipids and lipopeptides, each with unique characteristics and diverse applications in industries such as pharmaceuticals, agriculture, and food.</p></p>
